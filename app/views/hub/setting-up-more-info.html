Setting up your identity account

1. Select the identity account you want to use

Each identity account has different requirements, so we’ll now ask you some questions to filter out the accounts that won’t work for you.

When you select the account you want to use, we’ll send you to their website.

2. Confirm your details so they can verify your identity

The first first thing you will have to do is create a username and password. This is so you can reuse your identity account wherever you see the GOV.UK Verify logo.

You will need to provide some personal details, including identity documents if you have them, so they can double check them against records held by mobile phone providers, credit agencies, HM Passport Office or DVLA for example.

When you're identity account is set up, you will be sent back to GOV.UK so you can safely and securely {{serviceNameLower}}.

You only need to set up one identity account, next time you just sign in with your username and password.


Where can I use my identity account?

You can use your identity account to:

  • complete your Self Assessment
  • check your State Pension
  • sign in to your personal tax account
  • view or share your driving licence information
  • complete your claim or access your Universal Credit account
  • claim your redundancy payment and monies owed
  • check your income tax for the current year
  • report a medical condition that affects your driving
  • renew your short-term medical driving licence
  • check or update your company car tax
  • use Rural Payments
  • help friends and family with their tax
  • apply for a vehicle operator licence
  • request a basic DBS check
